About

The Land of Nalanda

Bihar is one of the oldest continuously inhabited regions of India — birthplace of great empires, religions and the world's earliest universities.

It is home to Nalanda University, Bodh Gaya, Rajgir, Vaishali, and the grandeur of Chhath Puja — a state where every stone has a story.

Famous Tourist Places

Bodh Gaya

UNESCO World Heritage Site where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment.

Nalanda University

The world's oldest residential university and a symbol of knowledge.

Rajgir

Ancient city famous for hot springs, ropeway and Vishwa Shanti Stupa.

Golghar

The iconic granary and one of Patna's most recognisable landmarks.

Takht Sri Patna Sahib

Birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh Ji.

Vaishali

Birthplace of Lord Mahavira and home to the Ashokan Pillar.

Valmiki Tiger Reserve

Bihar's only tiger reserve, with rich wildlife and dense forest.

Kesaria Stupa

One of the tallest Buddhist stupas in the world.
